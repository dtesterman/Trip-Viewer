Bar Harbor & Acadia National Park

October 11–16, 2026 · Darrell + wife · 5 nights

📅 Best alignment: Arriving Sunday Oct 11 puts Cadillac Mountain on a Tuesday (no weekend crowds) and the Seal Cove Auto Museum on a Wednesday (open daily through Oct 31). Most Bar Harbor restaurants stay open through mid-October — reserve dinners by early September to be safe.

Day 1

Arrival in Maine

Sunday, October 11

✈️
Morning – Afternoon DFW → BGR (connecting flight) Est. $300–400/person RT

No direct flights — most routes connect through DCA, CLT, or PHL. Plan for a full travel day; afternoon arrival in Bangor is typical.

🚗
Late Afternoon Pick up rental car at BGR Est. $50–70/day

Reserve a mid-size SUV or sedan. The drive from Bangor to Bar Harbor is about 50 miles / 1 hour on Route 1A — a pleasant introduction to coastal Maine.

🏨
Evening Check in — The Inn on Mount Desert (or similar) Est. $150–200/night

Charming B&B-style inn within walking distance of downtown Bar Harbor. Complimentary breakfast included. No Club Wyndham property available in the Bar Harbor area.

🍽️
7:00 PM Dinner — La Bella Vita Est. $70–90 for 2

Authentic Italian with flatbread pizza, antipasti, and handmade pastas. Earthy décor with Italian mosaics — a warm welcome after a travel day.

😴
Night Sleep — Bar Harbor Base lodging

💡 If your flight arrives early enough, take the slightly longer Route 3 through Trenton for water views on the approach to Mount Desert Island.

Day 2

Park Loop Road Scenic Drive

Monday, October 12

🗺️
9:00 AM Hulls Cove Visitor Center — Start Park Loop Road $15/vehicle (7-day pass)

Pick up a park map and get your bearings. The 27-mile loop is mostly one-way with frequent pull-offs — perfect for a leisurely half-day drive.

🌊
9:45 AM Sand Beach Free

A rare sandy beach tucked between granite headlands. Beautiful to view from the overlook — the water will be far too cold for swimming in October.

🌊
10:15 AM Thunder Hole Free

A natural sea cave that produces a thunderous boom when waves crash in at the right tide. Short paved walkway from the parking area to the viewing platform.

🌊
10:45 AM Otter Cliffs Overlook Free

Dramatic 110-foot granite cliffs plunging into the Atlantic. One of the most photographed spots in Acadia — easily viewed from the roadside pull-off.

🍽️
12:00 PM Lunch — Jordan Pond House Est. $50–70 for 2

The only restaurant inside the park, famous for warm popovers with butter and jam. Lawn seating overlooks Jordan Pond with the Bubbles mountains behind it. Reservations strongly recommended.

🍽️
6:30 PM Dinner — Mama DiMatteo's Est. $60–80 for 2

Regional Italian and New American cuisine. Handmade pastas and a good selection beyond seafood. Casual, welcoming atmosphere.

😴
Night Sleep — Bar Harbor Base lodging

💡 Reserve Jordan Pond House well in advance — it's the most popular lunch spot in Acadia and books up even in October. Check incoming tide times for Thunder Hole; the effect is best 1–2 hours before high tide.

Day 3

Cadillac Mountain & Bar Harbor Village

Tuesday, October 13

⛰️
9:00 AM Cadillac Mountain Summit Drive $6 vehicle reservation

Drive to the top of the highest point on the U.S. Atlantic coast (1,530 ft). 360-degree panorama of Frenchman Bay, the Porcupine Islands, and fall foliage in every direction. Paved summit area with interpretive signs.

🏛️
11:00 AM Abbe Museum (Downtown) $10/person

Smithsonian-affiliated museum showcasing 10,000 years of Wabanaki Native American history and culture. The largest collection of Maine Native American basketry in any museum. Allow 1–1.5 hours.

🛍️
1:00 PM Bar Harbor Village & Waterfront Free

Stroll the downtown shops along Cottage Street and Main Street. Walk the Shore Path along the waterfront for views of Frenchman Bay and the Porcupine Islands. Flat, paved, and easy walking.

🍽️
6:30 PM Dinner — The Royal Indian Est. $50–70 for 2

Modern Indian cuisine with seasonal ingredients — samosa pastries, curries, and naan. A welcome change of pace from the typical Maine fare.

😴
Night Sleep — Bar Harbor Base lodging

💡 Cadillac Mountain vehicle reservations must be booked at recreation.gov — 30% release 90 days out, 70% release 2 days before. Set a reminder for July 15 to book the 90-day window for Oct 13. Bring a jacket; it's always 10–15°F cooler at the summit.

Day 4

Quiet Side of Mount Desert Island

Wednesday, October 14

🚗
9:30 AM Seal Cove Auto Museum $8/person (senior)

America's premier collection of Brass Era automobiles (1886–1917) — early motorcycles, period clothing, and accessories too. About 30 minutes from Bar Harbor on the quiet western side of the island. Open 10 AM – 5 PM. Allow 1.5–2 hours.

🌿
12:00 PM Asticou Azalea Garden $5 donation

A 2.3-acre Japanese stroll garden with raked gravel paths, a great pond, and lily pond. In mid-October, the weeping hemlocks, birches, and maples blaze with red, orange, and gold. Flat, easy walking. Allow 45 min – 1 hour.

🛍️
1:30 PM Northeast Harbor Village Free

A quiet, upscale village with a picturesque marina, small galleries, and a few shops. Grab lunch at a local café — much less crowded than Bar Harbor.

🚗
2:30 PM Sargent Drive Scenic Road Free

A narrow, winding one-way road hugging Somes Sound — the only fjord on the U.S. Atlantic coast. Stunning water-and-mountain views from behind the wheel. Connects Northeast Harbor back toward the main road.

🍽️
6:30 PM Dinner — Testa's (farewell dinner) Est. $70–90 for 2

A Bar Harbor institution since 1934 serving Italian-American classics — steaks, pasta, and chicken alongside their seafood menu. White tablecloth feel without being stuffy.

😴
Night Sleep — Bar Harbor Base lodging

💡 Drive west to Seal Cove first, then loop east to Northeast Harbor and take Sargent Drive back. This routing avoids backtracking and gives you three distinct experiences — automotive history, Japanese garden serenity, and a coastal fjord drive.

Day 5

Schoodic Peninsula

Thursday, October 15

🚗
9:00 AM Drive to Schoodic Peninsula Free (covered by park pass)

About 1 hour from Bar Harbor via Route 1 and Route 186. This is the only mainland section of Acadia — far fewer visitors than Mount Desert Island. The 6-mile one-way loop road hugs the coastline.

🌊
10:15 AM Schoodic Point Free

A massive granite ledge jutting into the open Atlantic with panoramic views and waves crashing dramatically against the rocks. Short, flat walk from parking to the point.

🗺️
11:00 AM Blueberry Hill Overlook Free

A roadside pull-off with sweeping views of the Schoodic coastline. In October the low-bush blueberry fields turn brilliant crimson — gorgeous against the blue ocean.

🍽️
12:30 PM Lunch in Winter Harbor Est. $40–60 for 2

Small fishing village at the entrance to the Schoodic section. A few casual eateries for a relaxed lunch before heading back.

🛍️
4:00 PM Last stroll through Bar Harbor Free

Pick up souvenirs, browse the galleries, or just enjoy the town one last time. Relax before your farewell dinner.

🍽️
6:30 PM Dinner — Geddy's Est. $50–70 for 2

Family-friendly spot on Main Street with burgers, pizza, steak tips, and a wide variety beyond seafood. Relaxed atmosphere.

😴
Night Sleep — Bar Harbor Base lodging

💡 Schoodic is far less crowded than the main park, even in foliage season. The loop road is one-way and narrow in places — take your time and pull off at every overlook. Budget about 3 hours for the full Schoodic experience including the drive out.
