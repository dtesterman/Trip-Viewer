🎬 Galena to Woodstock Road Trip

Darrell + wife · 6 days, 5 nights · Jun 1–6, 2027 · Galena → Woodstock → O'Hare

📅 Scheduling notes: Anderson Japanese Gardens is open daily May–Oct. Historic Auto Attractions is closed Mondays (Day 4 is Friday — no conflict). Illinois Railway Museum runs diesel & electric trains on summer weekdays and adds steam on weekends — Day 4 is Friday, so confirm the Jun 4 schedule at irm.org/calendar. Day 1

Galena History & Grant Trail

Wednesday, June 2 · History & culture

☕
9:00 AM Breakfast at resort or Galena café

Easy morning. The day's attractions are all within a few minutes of each other in town.

🏛️
10:00 AM U.S. Grant Home State Historic Site Free

The Italianate home gifted to Grant by Galena citizens after the Civil War. Guided tours of the preserved interior.

🏛️
11:30 AM Galena & U.S. Grant Museum ~$15/pp

1858 Italianate mansion with 6,000 sq ft of exhibits on Galena's lead-mining history, the Civil War, and Grant's leather store. Features a real 1830s lead mine shaft in the basement.

🍽️
12:30 PM Lunch on Main Street

Plenty of options within a few blocks. Casual and walkable.

🏛️
2:00 PM Belvedere Mansion Est. ~$15/pp

1857 Italianate mansion with period furnishings and green drapes from Gone with the Wind. Guided tour of the interior.

🌊
3:30 PM Thunder Bay Falls Free

40-foot waterfall tucked into a wooded ravine just outside town. Short paved path from the parking area — no hiking required.

🍽️
Dinner Log Cabin Steakhouse

A Galena institution since 1937. Greek-influenced steaks and chops in a rustic log cabin setting.

😴
Night Club Wyndham Galena Sleep — Night 2

💡 Galena Trolley Tours offers a Tour of Homes ($38/pp, ~2.5 hrs) that includes house admissions — call 815-777-1248 to confirm it's offered in 2027 before planning around it.

Day 2

Chestnut Mountain Scenic Day

Thursday, June 3 · Nature & scenic

☕
9:00 AM Breakfast at resort

Easy morning before heading to Chestnut Mountain, about 20 minutes southeast of Galena.

🗻
10:30 AM Chestnut Mountain Resort — Chairlift Ride

Scenic chairlift up the bluff with panoramic views of the Mississippi River Valley. Drive-up accessibility at the base; the lift does the climbing for you.

🍽️
12:00 PM The Summit Food & Spirits

Perched atop Chestnut Mountain with Mississippi River Valley views from a 1,400 sq ft deck. Steaks, seafood, and signature sandwiches. Arrive by sunset for the best experience.

🚢
2:00 PM Mississippi River Cruise Est. ~$25–35/pp

1.5-hour narrated cruise from the resort dock. Wildlife, bluffs, and river history. Bald eagles and herons are common sightings along this stretch.

🗺️
5:00 PM Scenic Drive via Blackjack Road Free

Wind through the rolling hills of the Driftless Area on the way back to Galena. Pull-offs for photos of the valley.

🍽️
Dinner Dinner in Galena

Last evening in Galena. Pick from the Main Street restaurants or return to a favorite from the trip.

😴
Night Club Wyndham Galena Sleep — Night 3 (last Wyndham night)

💡 Early June is peak summer-launch season — chairlift and river cruise should both be running on their full schedule. Confirm times at chestnutmtn.com or call 815-777-1320.

Day 3

Rockford, Roscoe & Railway Museum → Woodstock

Friday, June 4 · Day trip east · Check into Cherry Tree Inn

🚗
8:00 AM Check out Club Wyndham — Drive to Rockford

~1.5 hours east on US-20. Rolling farmland gives way to the Rock River Valley.

🌿
9:30 AM Anderson Japanese Gardens ~$10–11/pp

Rated the highest-quality Japanese garden in North America. Koi ponds, waterfalls, stone paths, and tea house on 12 landscaped acres. Fully accessible on paved paths. Opens 9 AM weekends.

🍽️
11:15 AM Quick Lunch in Rockford

Fresco at the Gardens (if timing works) or a casual spot in the Rockford area before heading to Roscoe.

🏎️
12:00 PM Historic Auto Attractions — Roscoe $28/pp

86,000 sq ft of presidential limos, movie cars, and pop-culture artifacts. JFK's limo, Bonnie & Clyde's car, and hundreds more. Open Tue–Sun; closed Monday. ~15 min from Rockford.

🚗
2:00 PM Drive to Union

~1 hour east on I-90 toward Chicago, then south to Union. You're heading toward Woodstock — IRM is on the way.

🚂
3:00 PM Illinois Railway Museum $18/pp (senior)

North America's largest railway museum. In early June, diesel and electric trains run on weekdays. Ride vintage trains on the museum's own rail lines. Last train departs at 4 PM.

🚗
5:00 PM Drive to Woodstock

~20 minutes north to Woodstock. You'll arrive just in time to settle in before dinner.

🏨
5:30 PM Check in — Cherry Tree Inn B&B $238–279/night

The actual Victorian B&B from Groundhog Day — Bill Murray's character stayed here in the film. Each room includes a DVD player with a copy of the movie. Call to book; no online reservations.

🍽️
7:00 PM Dinner at Squire on the Square

The bar where Phil and Rita toast to world peace in the film. Right on the historic Square — your first taste of Punxsutawney, PA (Woodstock-style).

😴
Night Cherry Tree Inn B&B Sleep — Night 4 · Cherry Tree Inn

💡 IRM runs diesel & electric trains on summer weekdays; steam is added on weekends — Arrive by 3 PM to catch the last departure at 4 PM. The museum closes at 5 PM.

Day 4

🎬 Groundhog Day Walking Tour

Saturday, June 5 · Film locations & culture · No driving

☕
Morning B&B Breakfast at Cherry Tree Inn Included

Full breakfast included with your stay. Eat in the same house where Bill Murray filmed his morning scenes.

🎬
10:00 AM Woodstock Square Historic District Free

The heart of "Punxsutawney, PA" in the film. The 19th-century Victorian buildings, courthouse, and surrounding streets are largely unchanged since filming. Every corner is recognizable.

🎭
10:30 AM Woodstock Opera House Free (exterior)

Prominently featured in the film and a must-see for Groundhog Day fans. Recently renovated with a new bar, dressing rooms, and updated theatrical equipment. Check if tours or events are available during your visit.

🎨
11:00 AM Bill Murray Mural & Sculpture Garden Free

Charming alley at 229 Main St with a Groundhog Day mural of Bill Murray, a groundhog statue, and a statue of Orson Welles. Connected to the Main Street Pedway — a pedestrian alleyway through the downtown filming area.

🎬
11:30 AM Gobbler's Knob Free

Southwest corner of Woodstock Square Park — the "prediction" scene where Phil Connors covers the groundhog ceremony. A plaque marks the spot.

🎬
11:45 AM The Puddle Corner — 100 Cass Street Free

The corner where Phil Connors steps into the same icy puddle every morning. One of the most recognizable repeating gags in the film.

🍽️
12:30 PM Lunch at Public House of Woodstock

The bar where Rita and Phil get to know each other over sweet vermouths on the rocks with a twist. Right on the Square — an essential Groundhog Day stop.

🚶
Afternoon Explore the Square & Downtown Free

Revisit favorites, browse shops, or just sit and people-watch on the Square. The Cherry Tree Inn exterior is worth a photo from across the street — seeing it as a visitor, not a guest.

🍽️
Dinner Dinner in Woodstock

Casual dinner on or near the Square. Your last evening in Phil Connors' time loop.

😴
Night Cherry Tree Inn B&B Sleep — Night 5 · Cherry Tree Inn

💡 No driving today — walk everything. The entire Square is the film set. Take your time and enjoy it without rushing. The walking tour covers about half a mile total.
